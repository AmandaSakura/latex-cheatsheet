\section{L4: Naive Bayes, Vectors, SGNS, A1 Repro}
\subsection*{Multinomial Naive Bayes}
MAP classifier:
\[
\hat c_{MAP}=\arg\max_{c\in C}P(c\mid x).
\]
\[
\hat c_{MAP}=\arg\max_{c\in C}P(c)P(x\mid c).
\]
Multinomial likelihood for bag-of-words counts $x_i$:
\[
P(x\mid c)=\frac{n!}{\prod_{i=1}^{|V|}x_i!}
\prod_{i=1}^{|V|}\theta_{c,i}^{x_i},
\]
\[
n=\sum_i x_i.
\]
Log-space decision rule:
\[
\hat c=\arg\max_{c\in C}
\left[\log P(c)+\sum_{i=1}^{|V|}x_i\log\theta_{c,i}\right].
\]
MLE and add-$\alpha$ smoothing:
\[
\theta_{c,i}^{MLE}=\frac{n_{i,c}}{n_c},
\qquad
\theta_{c,i}=\frac{n_{i,c}+\alpha}{n_c+\alpha|V|}.
\]

\subsection*{Word Vectors and Similarity}
Dot product:
\[
a\cdot b=\sum_i a_i b_i.
\]
Norm and cosine:
\[
\|a\|=\sqrt{\sum_i a_i^2},
\qquad
\cos(a,b)=\frac{a\cdot b}{\|a\|\|b\|}.
\]
Use cosine when vector length/frequency bias is undesirable.

\subsection*{Window Size and Neighbor Type (L4)}
Small context window ($C=\pm2$): nearest neighbors are often syntactic/taxonomic.\\
Large context window ($C=\pm5$): neighbors shift toward topical/semantic field relations.\\
Lecture example (Hogwarts): small window gives fictional-school neighbors; large window gives Harry-Potter-world terms.

\subsection*{Analogy by Parallelogram}
For $a:a^{*} :: b:b^{*}$,\\
$v(b^{*}) \approx v(b)-v(a)+v(a^{*})$.\\
Canonical examples: $v(king)-v(man)+v(woman)\approx v(queen)$;\\
$v(Paris)-v(France)+v(Italy)\approx v(Rome)$.
- Caveat (L4 slide): not universal; works better for some relation types than others.\\
- Caveat (L4 slide): performance depends on frequent words and stable semantics.

\subsection*{Embedding Bias Notes}
Slides emphasize: embeddings reflect statistical regularities and cultural bias in training data.\\
Example template: father:doctor::mother:$x$ may return stereotyped completions.\\
Downstream retrieval/ranking/classification systems can inherit these biases if embeddings are used directly.

\subsection*{SGNS Core (lecture form)}
For target word $w$ and context word $c$:
\[
P(+\mid w,c)=\sigma(u_c\cdot v_w).
\]
\[
P(-\mid w,c)=\sigma(-u_c\cdot v_w).
\]
Two sets of embeddings in training: word vectors $v_w$ and context vectors $u_c$; final embedding often uses $v_w$ (or $v_w+u_w$).\\
One positive + $k$ negatives objective:
\[
\begin{array}{l}
L_{CE}= -\Big[
\log\sigma(u_{c^+}\cdot v_w)\\
\quad +\sum_{i=1}^{k}\log\sigma(-u_{c_i^-}\cdot v_w)
\Big].
\end{array}
\]
Useful derivatives:
\[
\frac{d}{d\alpha}\log\sigma(\alpha)=\sigma(-\alpha).
\]
\[
\frac{d}{d\alpha}\log\sigma(-\alpha)=-\sigma(\alpha).
\]

\]
not a valid global distribution across all lengths.\\

\subsection*{L4 Pitfalls}
- Compute NB in log space to avoid underflow.\\
- Without smoothing, one unseen word can zero a class score.\\
- Dot product alone can over-favor frequent words; cosine is safer.
